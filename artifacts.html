MonopolAR

MonopolAR is a game that I developed with a team of 4 for my GIMM 110 class at Boise State University. MonopolAR is essentially a game of Monopoly, but with a twist. The game is played through your phone camera, using the Unity AR Foundations package. With this, you can use your own monopoly board and play a virtual version with no setup required. The image target creates an anchor point that the virtual play area will be set on top of and then the game is played similar to normal monopoly. Getting a demo of the AR functionality is nearly impossible for me as of right now because it must be run on an Android device, but I have created a desktop version as well, which can be viewed on GitHub.

Skills: C#, AR Unity, basic 3D Modeling

Burglar Trap

Burglar Trap is a 2D game I made for my first GIMM project. In the game, you control the security system of a store, as burglars try to break in. Being set up as a tower defense, the player will control guns, grenades, and time in order to stop the hordes of enemies who follow the set path. Having some experience with Unity and C# from previous projects, I was challenged to take the development a step further. To do this, I implemented the library for a LeapMotion hand tracker, which will track the movements of your hands and create a skeletal map over what is tracked. This gives the player the ability to control aspects of the game with their gestures.

Skills: C#, LeapMotion, Unity, Illustrator

Buccaneers from Beyond

Buccaneers from Beyond is a fixed action 2D shooter, similar to Galaga. The player controls a pirate ship, the Friggerock, and must destroy the enemy ships, which are manned by Vikings. The idea may not seem very unique at first, but the gameplay gets very interesting. You are fighting in space, aboard a space "pirate ship" that must be upgraded through the resources collected by destroying the enemy ships. Upgrades include fuel capacity, ship integrity, cannon power, and thruster force. After surviving, you encounter Nordic beasts like the Kraken and Fenrir.

Skills: Unity, Aseprite, C#

GIMM Smart Chair

The smart chair project was one of the tasks assigned to my co-intern and I from our internship with the GimmWorks Development Team. We built a chair using two pressure sensors and an Arduino with WiFi. When a person sits, timestamps are recorded and sent to a website (originally hosted on Heroku) that displays session time. I also 3D modeled a protective box for the Arduino.

Skills: Arduino, TinkerCad, Resource Planning, Premier Pro

Parallel

Parallel is an interactive comic experience I created on a team of 4 in my second semester of the GIMM program. The game explains quantum entanglement through the use of mirror controls. The player controls characters A and B, who must solve puzzles to escape a ship overrun by alien ghosts. The project was completed in Unity and is hosted on GitHub.

Skills: Unity, Github, Illustrator, Team Brainstorming
