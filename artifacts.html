MonopolAR

MonopolAR is a game that I developed with a team of 4 for my GIMM 110 class at Boise State University. MonopolAR is essentially a game of Monopoly, but with a twist. The game is played through your phone camera, using the Unity AR Foundations package. With this, you can use your own monopoly board and play a virtual version with no setup required. The image target creates an anchor point that the virtual play area will be set on top of and then the game is played similar to normal monopoly. Getting a demo of the AR functionality is nearly impossible for me as of right now because it must be run on an Android device, but I have created a desktop version as well, which can be viewed on github.

Skills: C#, AR Unity, basic 3D Modeling

Burglar Trap

Burglar Trap is a 2D game I made for my first GIMM project. In the game, you control the security system of a store, as burglars try to break in. Being set up as a tower defense, the player will control guns, grenades, and time in order to stop the hordes of enemies who follow the set path. Having some experience with Unity and C# from previous projects, I was challenged to take the development a step further. To do this, I implemented the library for a LeapMotion hand tracker, which will track the movements of your hands and create a skeletal map over what is tracked. This gives the player the ability to control aspects of the game with their gestures. The two guns will follow the position of the player's hand and split if two hands are detected. If the player closes their fist to a certain point a grenade will detonate at the point being aimed at and if the player flips their hand upside down, the time will be frozen gradually. Finally, if the player makes an "ok" sign with their hand, time will begin to reverse.

Skills: C#, LeapMotion, Unity, Illustrator

Buccaneers from Beyond

Buccaneers from Beyond is a fixed action 2D shooter, similar to Galaga. The player controls a pirate ship, the Friggerock, and must destroy the enemy ships, which are manned by Vikings. The idea may not seem very unique at first, but the gameplay gets very interesting. For starters you are fighting in space, aboard a space "pirate ship" that must be upgraded through the resources collected by destroying the enemy ships. The four upgradable parts of the ship are the fuel capacity, ship integrity, cannon power and thruster force. Once the player has survived long enough, they will encounter one of the great Nordic beasts. Some of which include the Kraken and Fenrir. The player must destroy these beasts in order to progress to the next level. The game has no real end as the player can continue upgrading their ship and becoming more and more powerful. This game was made to compete in the SkillsUSA "Interactive Application and Video Game Design" competition, and I had a main partner, and some minor outside help. My official role on the team was the main artist, however, I also did the majority of the programming. Some of the concept art was made by someone who was not a part of the team. My partner and I won gold at the state level and competed at the national level. The game can be downloaded and viewed on GameJolt.

Skills: Unity, Aseprite, C#

GIMM Smart Chair

The smart chair project was one of the tasks assigned to my co-intern and I from our internship with the GimmWorks Development Team. The task was to create a chair that could detect when a person was sitting down. The chair was built using 2 highly sensitive pressure sensors and an arduino with WiFi capabilities. The arduino must be attached to the chair in some way; for our design I 3D modeled a box which the arduino would sit in. The design shown in the video was our final prototype which was used as inspiration for a GIMM IoT class (GIMM 340 I believe). Once a person sits on the chair, the arduino will record the timestamp at time of activation and when there is no more input (meaning they stand up) the arduino will record that time and calculate the time difference. The arduino will then send this data to a website hosted on Heroku, which will then display that information using the JavaScript charts and graphs library. The website was designed using PHP, HTML/CSS and JavaScript. Unfortunately, the website was shut down after the internship.

Skills: Arduino, TinkerCad, Resource Planning, Premier Pro
